Abandoned Chairs Inspo Reel

Forgotten Objects, Glass Memories.

A short motion reel exploring street chairs as fragile artifacts of over-consumption, memory, and urban infrastructure. Glass, bubble textures, and subtle wobble echo the instability of these everyday monuments.

Role · Direction, Design, 3D

CONCEPT

The reel collects visual “echoes” of abandoned chairs on New York streets, translating them into a glass-like digital form. Their transparency suggests both presence and absence, like NPCs in an open-world game.

The motion language is minimal and slightly unstable: a slow wobble, floating cubes, soft refractions. Nothing fully settles, mirroring how these objects hover between trash and treasure, function and memory.

Color stays restrained (deep navy, muted neons, subtle reflections) to highlight materiality and light. The sound design can be imagined as low, distant city hum with small percussive glitches.
